Our Team

Information about us.

Ground Zero Robotics is made up of Southwest Michigan high school students with a variety of backgrounds and interests. Some are homeschooled; others attend public school. Some are blacksmiths; others are wordsmiths. Some fiddle with electronics, others with coding. All of them share the same desire: to help design, build and program the best robot possible in an environment of mutual respect and creative enjoyment.

FIRST Robotics build season starts in early January with a reveal of the new “game.” Then the team has six weeks to create the robot before “Bag and Tag” day in late February. District competitions begin shortly after, and points earned at these competitions determine qualification for Michigan State Finals in April. A strong finish at State Finals can earn the team a place at International finals. (For more information on FIRST Robotics in Michigan, click here.)

Ground Zero meets regularly in the off-season to learn new skills, serve their community and raise funds for build season. The team loves sharing their lab, their robot and their enthusiasm with visitors. To schedule a visit or inquire about joining the team, contact the Head Coach Meg Edwards at [PUT EMAIL HERE].

Also, check out the Michiana Youth Robotics and Engineering website for information about similar opportunities.
